7 Pillars of Wellbeing: Self-Assessment

Rate each statement on a scale from 1 (Never) to 5 (Always) unless otherwise indicated.

1. Movement

I engage in physical movement (e.g., walking, yoga, dancing, workouts) at least 3-5 times a week.

I enjoy the physical activities I choose to do.

I feel strong, flexible, and energized.

I know how to stretch or strengthen specific muscles in my body when I feel pain or tightness. (Knowledge)

I have access to a safe and affordable space to move or exercise (e.g., gym, park, studio, home). (Access)

/ 25

2. Meditation

I practice meditation, mindfulness, or breathwork at least once a week.

I feel more focused and calm after these practices.

I know how to meditate or use breath to relax when I feel overwhelmed. (Knowledge)

I have time and a quiet space to meditate or reflect regularly. (Access)

/ 20

3. Nutrition

I eat a balanced diet that supports my energy and well-being.

I hydrate throughout the day.

I limit processed food and excess sugar.

I understand my personal protein needs and how to meet them through my diet. (Knowledge)

I know how to prepare healthy meals that nourish me. (Knowledge/Practice)

I have access to affordable, healthy food options. (Access)

4. Mental Wellness

I can manage stress in healthy ways.

My relationships feel emotionally supportive.

My work or study environment is not toxic.

I am not often overwhelmed by depression or anxiety.

I feel hopeful and motivated.

I know who to reach out to for mental health support if needed. (Knowledge/Access)

/ 30

5. Relaxation

I get enough restful sleep (7-9 hours per night).

I make time for rest, hobbies, or joy.

I unplug from screens to recharge.

I know what helps me personally unwind and relax. (Knowledge)

I have the time and space in my life for rest. (Access)

6. Connection

I feel a sense of belonging in my community.

I have someone I can speak to openly.

I give and receive support in relationships.

I know how to ask for help when I need it. (Knowledge/Practice)

I have access to social spaces, events, or circles where I can connect with others.

/ 25

7. Nature

I spend time in nature regularly

Being in nature helps me feel grounded.

I bring nature into my home/workspace (plants, fresh air, sunlight).

I know the benefits of spending time in nature

I have access to safe natural environments nearby
